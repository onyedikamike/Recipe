Afang Soup

Description

Afang soup is a tasty, nutritious Nigerian soup, native to the Efiks/Ibibios (Akwa Ibom & Cross river states).

Ingredients

  • Beef
  • Fish
  • Palm oil
  • Crayfish
  • Pepper
  • Shaki (cow tripe)
  • Waterleaf
  • Okazi leaf
  • Onion
  • Periwinkle
  • Salt
  • Some seasoning

Steps

  1. Just go ahead and start parboiling the meat with all the necessary ingredients (2 cubes of knorr, half cup of sliced onions, salt and other spice of choice)
  2. Some people wash the water leaves before slicing them while others choose to slice them before washing; I prefer the latter. Slice and wash the water leaves and set aside in a plastic sieve to make sure that water is properly drained away.
  3. Most people donâ€™t like a trace of water in their afang soup, that can be easily arranged, just try to squeeze the water leaves tightly before you add them to the soup.
  4. Soak the dried fish with warm water, remove the center bones and wash thoroughly with just water; also wash the stockfish head with warm water.
  5. Add them to the cooking meat and cook till they are soft and the pot is almost dry.
  6. Add the palm oil, stir all together.
  7. Add a seasoning cube, ground pepper, snails and salt to taste
  8. Allow cooking for 3-5 minutes.
  9. Add the water-leaves; allow to simmer for 5 minutes before adding the periwinkles, ground ukazi, and ground crayfish Stir all together
  10. cover the pot and allow simmering for another 5 minutes, stirring every 2 minutes
